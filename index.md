# Nutrition Plan

### Goal:
Small calorie deficit (~300–400 kcal), not aggressive.

## **Protein (most important)**

Going by ~1.6–2 g per kg body weight will result in~110–130 g/day approx. 
**Sources:**
- Chicken breast
- Eggs
- Paneer / tofu
- Greek yogurt
- Dal + rice combo

## **Carbs (don’t cut too low)**

Needed for workouts
**Prefer:**
- Rice (controlled portions)
- Roti
- Oats
- Fruits

## **Fats**

Moderate intake:
- Nuts
- Seeds
- Minimal cooking oil (1–2 tbsp/day max)

---

## **Simple Meal Structure**

### Breakfast
- Eggs + toast OR oats + yogurt + fruit
### Lunch (prep-friendly for your schedule)
- 150–200g chicken / paneer
- Rice or 2 rotis
- Vegetables
### Evening snack
- Fruit + handful nuts OR protein shake
### Dinner
- Protein-heavy + veggies
- Reduce carbs slightly at night

---
- **Start lighter than you think**  
    → You should finish a set feeling like you could do **2–3 more reps** (not exhausted)
- **Prioritize form over weight**  
    → Especially for: squats, lunges, RDLs, hip thrusts  
    → Poor form = wrong muscles + higher injury risk
- **Train consistently (not aggressively)**  
    → 5 days/week is fine, but don’t go all-out every session
- **Feel the target muscle**  
    → For glutes: squeeze at top, slow control  
    → If only thighs burn → adjust form
- **Rest properly**  
    → 60–90 sec between sets  
    → Sleep matters more than extra workouts

# How to Increase Load (Simple Progression)

### Step 1: Learn movement (Week 1–2)

- Use **very light weights or bodyweight**
- Focus: balance, control, full range of motion

### Step 2: Add small weight (Week 2–4)

- Pick a weight where:
    - You can do **10–12 reps comfortably**
    - Last 2 reps feel slightly challenging

### Step 3: Double progression method (easy to follow)

For each exercise:
- Target: **8–12 reps**
- Rule:
	- If you can do **12 reps easily → increase weight next session**
	- If you struggle below 8 reps → reduce weight

### Step 4: Increase gradually

- Add:
    - **+2.5 kg (upper body)**
    - **+5 kg (lower body)**
- Or even smaller jumps if needed

### Step 5: Track one thing

- Either:
    - Weight lifted
    - OR reps done
- Progress = either goes up over time
